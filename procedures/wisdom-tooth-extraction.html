Wisdom Tooth Extraction Protocol

Comprehensive surgical protocol for safe and effective third molar removal with optimal patient outcomes.

Procedure Overview

Duration

45-90 minutes depending on complexity

Anesthesia

Local anesthesia with optional sedation

Difficulty Level

Moderate to Advanced

Recovery Time

7-14 days for complete healing

Important Considerations

Always assess radiographs for proximity to inferior alveolar nerve, maxillary sinus, and adjacent teeth. Consider patient's medical history and contraindications before proceeding.

Pre-operative Assessment

  1. 1

    Medical History Review

    Complete medical and dental history including current medications, allergies, bleeding disorders, and previous surgical complications. Assess ASA classification and obtain medical clearance if necessary.

  2. 2

    Radiographic Evaluation

    Review panoramic radiograph and consider CBCT for complex cases. Assess tooth position, root morphology, proximity to vital structures (IAN, maxillary sinus), and bone density.

  3. 3

    Clinical Examination

    Evaluate mouth opening, periodontal status, adjacent teeth, and soft tissue conditions. Document any signs of infection, cysts, or pathology.

  4. 4

    Informed Consent

    Discuss procedure details, risks (nerve injury, dry socket, bleeding, infection), benefits, alternatives, and post-operative instructions. Obtain written consent.

Surgical Procedure

  1. 1

    Patient Preparation

    Position patient comfortably, drape surgical site, and establish IV access if sedation is planned. Confirm emergency equipment availability.

  2. 2

    Anesthesia Administration

    Administer appropriate local anesthetic (2% lidocaine with 1:100,000 epinephrine). For mandibular third molars: IAN block, long buccal, and lingual infiltration. For maxillary: posterior superior alveolar and greater palatine blocks.

  3. 3

    Soft Tissue Management

    Design appropriate flap (envelope or triangular) based on tooth position and access requirements. Make clean incisions with sharp blade, reflect full-thickness mucoperiosteal flap.

  4. 4

    Bone Removal

    Remove bone using high-speed bur with copious irrigation. Create adequate access while preserving maximum bone. Remove bone around crown and cervical area as needed.

  5. 5

    Tooth Sectioning (if required)

    Section multi-rooted teeth or impacted teeth as necessary. Divide crown from roots or separate roots using high-speed bur with irrigation. Remove sections carefully.

  6. 6

    Tooth Removal

    Elevate tooth/root segments using appropriate elevators and forceps. Apply controlled force in proper direction. Avoid excessive force that could fracture adjacent teeth or alveolar bone.

  7. 7

    Socket Management

    Irrigate socket thoroughly, remove debris and granulation tissue. Smooth sharp bone edges, achieve hemostasis. Inspect for complete tooth removal and foreign bodies.

  8. 8

    Wound Closure

    Reposition flap and suture with 3-0 or 4-0 silk or vicryl. Ensure primary closure without tension. Place horizontal mattress or simple interrupted sutures as appropriate.

Post-operative Management

  1. 1

    Immediate Post-op

    Apply pressure with gauze for 30-45 minutes. Provide ice pack for first 24 hours (20 minutes on, 20 minutes off). Monitor for bleeding and ensure patient is stable before discharge.

  2. 2

    Pain Management

    Prescribe appropriate analgesics: Ibuprofen 600mg q6h + acetaminophen 650mg q6h. Consider stronger opioids for severe pain. Provide clear instructions for medication use.

  3. 3

    Infection Prevention

    Consider antibiotic prophylaxis if indicated (immunocompromised, extensive surgery, signs of infection). Typical regimen: Amoxicillin 500mg TID for 7 days or Clindamycin 300mg QID if penicillin allergic.

  4. 4

    Follow-up Care

    Schedule follow-up at 1 week for suture removal and healing assessment. Earlier if complications arise. Provide emergency contact information and clear instructions for urgent care.

Potential Complications & Management

Common Complications

  • Dry Socket (Alveolar Osteitis): Irrigate gently, place medicated dressing, prescribe pain medication
  • Nerve Injury: Document extent, refer to oral surgeon if permanent, consider steroid therapy
  • Excessive Bleeding: Apply pressure, suture if necessary, consider hemostatic agents
  • Infection: Drainage, antibiotic therapy, warm saltwater rinses
  • Sinus Communication: Evaluate size, primary closure vs. sinus precautions
