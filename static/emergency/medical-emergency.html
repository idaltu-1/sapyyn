Critical: Call 911 Immediately

For any life-threatening emergency including cardiac arrest, respiratory distress, severe allergic reactions, or loss of consciousness - activate emergency medical services immediately.

Basic Life Support (BLS) Protocol

  1. 1

    Check Responsiveness

    Tap shoulders firmly and shout "Are you okay?" If no response, call for help and activate emergency response system (911). Get AED if available.

  2. 2

    Check Pulse and Breathing

    Check carotid pulse for no more than 10 seconds. Look for normal breathing. If no pulse or abnormal breathing, begin CPR immediately.

  3. 3

    Position Patient

    Place patient on firm, flat surface. Tilt head back, lift chin to open airway. Remove any visible foreign objects from mouth.

  4. 4

    Begin Chest Compressions

    Place heel of hand on lower half of breastbone. Compress hard and fast at least 2 inches deep at rate of 100-120/min. Allow complete recoil between compressions.

  5. 5

    Provide Rescue Breathing

    Give 2 breaths after every 30 compressions. Each breath should last 1 second and make chest rise. Use bag-mask if available.

  6. 6

    Continue CPR

    Continue 30:2 compression to ventilation ratio until EMS arrives, AED becomes available, or patient shows signs of life. Switch compressors every 2 minutes to prevent fatigue.

Common Medical Emergencies

  1. 1

    Syncope (Fainting)

    Position patient supine with legs elevated. Ensure airway is clear. Administer oxygen if available. Monitor vital signs. If patient doesn't recover within 2-3 minutes, call 911.

  2. 2

    Allergic Reaction/Anaphylaxis

    Remove allergen if known. For mild reactions: antihistamine. For severe reactions: epinephrine auto-injector (EpiPen), call 911, administer oxygen, IV fluids if available.

  3. 3

    Seizures

    Clear area of hazards. Do NOT restrain patient or put objects in mouth. Time seizure duration. Position on side when convulsions stop. Call 911 if seizure lasts >5 minutes or patient has no history of seizures.

  4. 4

    Chest Pain/Heart Attack

    Call 911 immediately. Give aspirin 325mg (if not allergic). Position patient comfortably, usually sitting up. Administer oxygen. Monitor vital signs. Prepare for possible cardiac arrest.

  5. 5

    Respiratory Distress

    Position patient upright or in position of comfort. Administer high-flow oxygen. Remove any foreign objects if visible. For asthma: bronchial inhaler. Call 911 for severe distress.

  6. 6

    Diabetic Emergency

    If conscious: give oral glucose/sugar if hypoglycemic. If unconscious: call 911, administer glucagon if available and trained. Monitor airway and breathing. Do not give oral fluids to unconscious patient.

Emergency Medications

Emergency Drug Kit

Essential medications to have available: Epinephrine, Albuterol inhaler, Nitroglycerin, Aspirin, Glucose gel, Diphenhydramine, Hydrocortisone, Atropine
